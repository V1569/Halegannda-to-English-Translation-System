What is Halegannada?

Halegannada (‡≤π‡≤≥‡≥Ü‡≤ó‡≤®‡≥ç‡≤®‡≤°), also known as Old Kannada, is the ancestor of the modern Kannada language. It was predominantly used between the 6th and 14th centuries CE, during which it produced a rich body of literature encompassing poetry, prose, and various other forms of written expression.

This ancient form of Kannada was the language of royal courts, scholarly discourse, and literary excellence, giving us works by renowned poets like Pampa, Ranna, and Ponna, collectively known as the "Three Gems of Kannada Literature."

Our Mission

Our mission is to make Halegannada accessible to modern readers by providing accurate translations and preserving the cultural heritage embedded in these ancient texts. We aim to:

  • Bridge the gap between ancient and modern Kannada
  • Preserve and promote Kannada literary heritage
  • Make historical texts accessible to modern readers
  • Support scholars and enthusiasts in their study of classical Kannada literature
